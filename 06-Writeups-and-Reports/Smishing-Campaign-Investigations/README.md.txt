# Smishing Campaign Investigation

## Project Overview
This cybersecurity write-up documents the investigation of a fraudulent SMS-based smishing attack impersonating Indiana traffic enforcement. The message used legal intimidation tactics, an official-looking notice, and a malicious QR code in an attempt to coerce immediate victim payment.

## Investigation Summary
On May 2, 2026, an unsolicited text message was received from a suspicious sender containing a fabricated State of Indiana traffic enforcement image. Due to the urgent legal language, QR payment demand, and lack of legitimate verification channels, the message was treated as a live smishing attempt and subjected to further analysis.

## Threat Analysis
This campaign demonstrates several common modern phishing techniques:

- Government authority impersonation
- Legal urgency and fear-based compliance
- QR-code phishing (quishing)
- Mobile payment redirection
- Personal information harvesting

## Skills Demonstrated

- QR code destination validation
- Threat intelligence documentation
